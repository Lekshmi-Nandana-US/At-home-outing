Hyderabadi Chicken Biryani

A royal dish of fragrant basmati rice and marinated chicken cooked in layers using the "Dum" method.

Prep Time
40 Mins
Cook Time
45 Mins
Servings
4-5 People

Ingredients

  • 500g Basmati Rice (soaked)
  • 500g Chicken (bone-in)
  • 1 cup Fried Onions (Birista)
  • 1 cup Yogurt (Curd)
  • 2 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • Whole Spices (Cardamom, Cloves, Bay Leaf)
  • Fresh Mint & Coriander leaves
  • Saffron soaked in warm milk
  • 2 tbsp Ghee (Clarified Butter)

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Chicken:
    In a large bowl, mix chicken, yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, salt, half of the fried onions, chopped mint/coriander, and chili powder. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes (overnight is best).
  2. Par-boil the Rice:
    Boil a large pot of water with whole spices (bay leaf, cloves) and salt. Add the soaked rice. Cook only until 70% done (the grain should still be slightly hard in the center). Drain immediately.
  3. Layering (The Assembly):
    In a heavy-bottomed pot, spread the marinated chicken at the bottom. Layer the 70% cooked rice over the chicken.
  4. Add Aromatics:
    Top the rice with the remaining fried onions, fresh mint, ghee, and the saffron milk for color.
  5. Dum Cooking (Steaming):
    Seal the pot tightly with dough or foil and a heavy lid to trap steam. Cook on high heat for 5 minutes, then lowest heat for 20-25 minutes.
  6. Serve:
    Let it rest for 10 minutes, then gently fluff the rice with a fork to mix the layers.
