Classic Cr√®me Br√ªl√©e

A rich, vanilla bean custard topped with a contrasting layer of hard, caramelized sugar.

Prep Time
15 Mins
Cook Time
40 Mins
Servings
4 Ramekins

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Heavy Cream
  • 5 large Egg Yolks
  • 1/2 cup Granulated Sugar
  • 1 tsp Vanilla Extract (or 1 bean)
  • Pinch of Salt
  • Extra Sugar for topping
  • Hot water (for baking bath)

Instructions

  1. Heat the Cream:
    Preheat oven to 325¬∞F (160¬∞C). In a saucepan, heat the heavy cream and salt over medium heat until it just starts to simmer. Do not let it boil over. Remove from heat and stir in vanilla.
  2. Whisk Yolks:
    In a separate bowl, whisk egg yolks and sugar together until the mixture is pale yellow and slightly thick.
  3. Temper the Eggs (Crucial):
    Slowly pour about 1/4 cup of the hot cream into the egg mixture while whisking constantly (this prevents the eggs from scrambling). Then, slowly pour the rest of the cream in while whisking.
  4. The Water Bath:
    Place 4 ramekins in a deep baking dish. Divide the custard evenly into the ramekins. Pour hot water into the baking dish until it reaches halfway up the sides of the ramekins.
  5. Bake:
    Bake for 35-40 minutes. The centers should still be slightly jiggly when you shake the pan gently.
  6. Chill:
    Remove ramekins from the water and let them cool. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours (or overnight) to set properly.
  7. Caramelize:
    Just before serving, sprinkle a thin, even layer of sugar on top of the custard. Use a kitchen torch to melt the sugar until it turns dark amber and hardens. (If you don't have a torch, use your oven broiler for 1-2 minutes, watching closely!).
