Traditional Miso Soup

Simple, comforting, and essential to any Japanese meal. A warm bowl of umami goodness.

Prep Time
5 Mins
Cook Time
10 Mins
Servings
4 Bowls

Ingredients

  • 4 cups Dashi Stock (or vegetable broth)
  • 3-4 tbsp Miso Paste (White or Red)
  • 1 block Silken Tofu (cubed)
  • 1 tbsp Dried Wakame (Seaweed)
  • 1 Green Onion (finely chopped)

Instructions

  1. Prepare Ingredients:
    Cut the silken tofu into small 1cm cubes. Rehydrate the dried Wakame in a small bowl of water for 5 minutes, then drain.
  2. Heat the Stock:
    Pour the Dashi stock into a saucepan and bring it to a boil.
  3. Add Solids:
    Add the tofu cubes and the rehydrated wakame to the boiling stock. Simmer gently for 1 minute.
  4. Dissolve Miso (Crucial Step):
    Turn off the heat (or reduce to very low). Miso should not be boiled, or it loses flavor and probiotics. Place the miso paste in a ladle, dip it into the soup, and whisk it inside the ladle until it dissolves into the soup.
  5. Serve:
    Pour into bowls immediately. Garnish with chopped green onions. Serve hot.
